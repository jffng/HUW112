## **Course Description**

This course introduces students to the field of new media technology. For our purposes, new media are forms of communication and ways of expressing made possible by a computer. We will study and practice various foundational modes of digital media — text, images, graphics, videos, sound, and websites. 

We will look at how different types of people — artists, creatives, gamers — are creating with computers to express themselves and influence the world around them.

This is both a lecture and studio class. Most classes will contain some lecture, followed by some time to practice and work on your assignments and projects. We will learn together and share what we learn.

By the end of this course students will have a deeper understanding of the role that computational media plays in how we communicate, in addition to practical skills that allow you apply them.

## **Goals**

The course has three goals:

1. to introduce you to the field of new media.
2. to equip you with hands-on skills in this field to realize your ideas.
3. to help you develop your own critical and creative understanding of new media, and figure out which media you enjoy working with and want to pursue further.

## **Objectives**

On completion of this course, you will be able to:

1. Edit text on a computer.
2. Create and manipulate images using a graphical and/or code-based interface.
3. Edit video, sound, and animations.
4. Code a basic website by hand with HTML and CSS.
5. Explain your reasons for using a digital tool.
6. Showcase projects you made in class via an online portfolio.

*Having objectives allows us to evaluate how well we have achieved our goals.*

## Course Overview

### Unit 1 — Foundations of New Media

*March 5 – March 17*

An introduction to the field of new media and to the tools and habits we will use all semester. 

We will look at text and typing as a foundational way to interact with computers, and also a creative medium.

**Tools:** macOS (Finder, Spotlight), TextEdit, VS Code, terminal

***

### Unit 2 — Images and 2D

*March 19 – April 14*

How do computers see? How does looking through them influence how we see ourselves? What are the possibilities for editing and manipulating images?

We will look at the history of digital images, understand the differences between graphic formats, and practice creating and manipulating them.

**Tools:** Adobe Photoshop, p5.js

***

### Unit 3 — Video & Audio

*April 16 – April 30*

How is a video different than a movie? What happens when everyone has a video camera?  How do sounds affect us compared to visuals?  

We will look at how to create and manipulate time-based media — how to create and edit text, images, and sounds in combination to create video and audio. 

**Tools:** Adobe Premiere, GarageBand or Audacity, Koala Sampler  

***

### Unit 4 — Websites

*May 5 – May 19*

What is a website made of? Is Instagram a website? What happens when suddenly billions of people across the world can talk to each other instantly via the Internet?

We will look at the Internet, and how people put things there, and what is possible.

**Tools:** VS Code, HTML, CSS, WordPress

***

### Unit 5 — 3D graphics

*May 21 – June 2*

What happens when we try to bring our bodies into the computer? How can we represent the world around us, in virtual space?  

We will look at 3D graphics and virtual environments.

**Tools:** Adobe After Effects, Blender, other

***

### Unit 6 — Final Projects & Presentations

*June 4 – June 16*

We have asked a lot of question and looked at a lot of things.

You will take what you have learned and combine it to create a final project.
